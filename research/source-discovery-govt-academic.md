## Key Academic Papers

| Paper | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| **Acemoglu & Restrepo** (2020) | Each robot per 1,000 workers reduces employment ratio by 0.2% |
| **Brynjolfsson et al.** (2023/25) | AI assistance increases productivity 15% avg; biggest gains for less experienced |
| **St. Louis Fed** (2025) | 0.57 correlation GenAI adoption ↔ unemployment |
| **Dallas Fed** (2025) | High AI-exposure jobs: -13% employment for ages 22-25 |
| **Hartley et al.** (2025) | LLM adoption 30.1%→38.3%; 15-50% task-completion time reductions |
| **Humlum & Vestergaard** (2025) | Zero effects on earnings/hours in Denmark through 2024 |

**Key finding**: Task-level productivity gains of 15-50% well-documented, but aggregate employment effects ambiguous through 2025. Freelancers and entry-level workers show clearest displacement.

## Regional Coverage Ranking

1. **United States** — WARN + NY AI disclosure + Challenger + BLS + Fed research
2. **EU/Eurostat** — 27-country structured dataset, API
3. **Canada** — Statistics Canada quarterly AI surveys (exemplary)
4. **United Kingdom** — ONS fortnightly BICS
5. **Australia** — Strong reports, less frequent
6. **Japan/South Korea** — Good OECD reports
7. **India** — High impact, thin official data (50-63K IT jobs lost 2025)
8. **China** — Significant impact, limited access (22% drop in graduate job postings)
9. **Middle East** — Emerging, growth narrative dominates
10. **Latin America** — International body coverage only
11. **Africa** — Minimal, structurally limited

### Critical Gap: NY WARN AI checkbox is the single most important new data source globally — mandatory, structured, employer-reported. If other states follow, transformative.
