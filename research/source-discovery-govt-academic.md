## Key Academic Papers

| Paper | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| **Acemoglu & Restrepo** (2020) | Each robot per 1,000 workers reduces employment ratio by 0.2% |
| **Brynjolfsson et al.** (2023/25) | AI assistance increases productivity 15% avg; biggest gains for less experienced |
| **St. Louis Fed** (2025) | 0.57 correlation GenAI adoption ↔ unemployment |
| **Dallas Fed** (2025) | High AI-exposure jobs: -13% employment for ages 22-25 |
| **Hartley et al.** (2025) | LLM adoption 30.1%→38.3%; 15-50% task-completion time reductions |
| **Humlum & Vestergaard** (2025) | Zero effects on earnings/hours in Denmark through 2024 |

**Key finding**: Task-level productivity gains of 15-50% well-documented, but aggregate employment effects ambiguous through 2025. Freelancers and entry-level workers show clearest displacement.

## European Collective Redundancy Framework

All EU/EEA countries implement **Directive 98/59/EC** on collective redundancies, which requires:
- Employers to notify competent public authority when dismissing 20+ employees within 90 days
- 30-day waiting period before dismissals take effect
- Worker consultation obligations

This makes every EU country's employment service a potential source of layoff notification data, though **publication and accessibility vary enormously**. Best public data availability:

1. **UK** (HR1 data published weekly by ONS)
2. **Sweden** (varsel statistics published monthly by Arbetsformedlingen)
3. **France** (PSE data published quarterly by DARES)
4. **Spain** (ERE data published by Ministry of Labour)
5. **Germany** (aggregate stats by Bundesagentur fur Arbeit, but individual notifications not published)

### Priority Scraping Targets for Layoff Tracking
- UK ONS HR1 dataset (structured, weekly)
- Swedish Arbetsformedlingen varsel data (structured, monthly)
- French DARES licenciements collectifs (quarterly, downloadable)
- Spanish MITES ERE statistics (monthly bulletins)
- Italian INPS Cassa Integrazione observatory (monthly)
- Eurofound EU Restructuring Monitor (event-level, all EU countries)

## Regional Coverage Assessment (Americas, Middle East, Africa)

### Data Quality & Accessibility Ranking

**Tier 1 — Structured, frequent, accessible:**
- Canada (StatCan LFS monthly, API)
- Brazil (IBGE PNAD + MTE CAGED monthly)
- Israel (CBS monthly LFS, API)
- South Africa (Stats SA QLFS + retrenchment notifications)
- Saudi Arabia (GASTAT quarterly, open data)

**Tier 2 — Regular surveys, downloadable:**
- Mexico (INEGI ENOE quarterly + IMSS monthly)
- Chile (INE ENE monthly)
- Colombia (DANE GEIH monthly)
- Argentina (INDEC EPH quarterly)
- UAE, Qatar, Bahrain (quarterly/annual reports)
- Morocco (HCP quarterly)
- Kenya, Ghana, Nigeria (periodic surveys, improving)

**Tier 3 — Periodic, limited online access:**
- Peru, Ecuador, Uruguay, Costa Rica, Dominican Republic
- Jordan, Egypt, Iran
- Tunisia, Algeria, Rwanda, Tanzania, Ethiopia

**Tier 4 — Irregular or unreliable:**
- Venezuela (data publication disrupted since 2016)
- Cuba (state-controlled, limited access)
- Bolivia, Paraguay, Honduras, Guatemala, El Salvador, Nicaragua
- Iraq, Lebanon (conflict/crisis disruption)
- Most Sub-Saharan Africa outside SA/Kenya/Nigeria/Ghana

### Key Layoff Tracking Sources (non-European)
- **Brazil MTE CAGED** — Monthly formal sector hiring/firing by sector. Closest to WARN in the Americas
- **South Africa Section 189/189A** — Mandatory retrenchment notifications for 50+ employees
- **Mexico IMSS** — Monthly formal employment registrations show net job losses by sector
- **Canada StatCan LFS** — Monthly with AI adoption overlay (6% of AI firms reduced employment)
- **Israel CBS** — Monthly LFS, tech sector granularity
- **GCC-Stat / GLMM** — Cross-GCC employment by nationality/sector
