## Notes on Coverage Gaps

1. **Africa** — Very limited AI layoff tracking. Best bets: TechCabal (techcabal.com), Disrupt Africa (disruptafrica.com), IT News Africa (itnewsafrica.com)
2. **Latin America** — Limited English coverage. Best bets: TechCrunch en Espanol, Convergencia Digital (Brazil), iProUP (Argentina)
3. **Middle East** — Gulf News (gulfnews.com) covers tech layoffs regionally; Arabian Business (arabianbusiness.com) for GCC
4. **Russia/CIS** — Limited access due to geopolitics. CNews.ru covers Russian IT sector
5. **Non-English datasets** — Most structured layoff datasets are English-only. Country-specific labor ministry data is the best non-English source
6. **Real-time company headcount** — Revelio Labs and Live Data Technologies are the closest to real-time tracking via LinkedIn profile change analysis
7. **AI-attributed vs. total layoffs** — Challenger data shows only 4.5% of 2025 layoffs were explicitly AI-attributed (54,836 of 1.17M). Many AI-driven cuts are reported under "restructuring" or "cost-cutting"
