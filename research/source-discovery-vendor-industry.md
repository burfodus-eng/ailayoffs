## Blind Spots (Hidden Displacement)

### 1. Contractor/Freelancer Displacement
- **Ramp data**: Freelancer spend dropped from 0.66% to 0.14% of company spend (Q4 2021→Q3 2025)
- **Academic**: GenAI cut demand for substitutable freelance skills by up to 50%
- Over half of businesses using freelancers in 2022 have stopped entirely

### 2. Soft Attrition (Not Replacing Departures)
- **Anthropic research**: Hiring rates for 22-25 year-olds in high-exposure occupations have measurably slowed
- No formal layoff announcement needed — just stop backfilling

### 3. AI-Washing (Using AI as Justification)
- 95% of companies investing in AI saw zero measurable profit impact (MIT)
- 55% of organizations that did AI layoffs now regret it
- Sam Altman: "there's some AI washing where people are blaming AI for layoffs they would otherwise do"

### 4. Hours Reduction
- No definitive single source — genuine blind spot
- Monitor BLS average weekly hours by sector

### 5. Outsourcing Enabled by AI
- AI enables smaller teams in low-cost countries
- Philippines/India BPO workers being asked to train AI systems that replace them

## Historical Backfill

### Recommended Start Date: January 2023
ChatGPT released Nov 2022; IBM's May 2023 announcement was the watershed moment.

### Cumulative Totals
- **2023**: ~24,000 AI-attributed (Challenger)
- **2024**: ~12,700 AI-attributed (Challenger — likely underreported)
- **2025**: ~55,000 (Challenger) / 100K+ (Programs.com)
- **2026 YTD**: 30,000+
- **Cumulative**: 92K (Challenger) to 156K+ (JobGoneToAI)

### Best Historical Sources
1. Challenger Gray & Christmas monthly reports (AI as category since 2023)
2. LayoffData.com WARN database (81K+ notices)
3. JobGoneToAI.com (156,499 jobs across 58 companies)
4. Programs.com AI Layoffs list (100K+ affected)
5. Anthropic Labor Market Index (novel "observed exposure" metric)
